I'm a freelance bilingual data and graphics journalist with over a decade of experience covering the Latino community in the USA. Previously, as part of Univision Noticias' Graphics team, I was responsible for creating charts, maps, and other data visualizations. Our work was recognized with multiple awards, including honors from the Radio Television Digital News Association (Edward. R Murrow Award), the NPPA (Portfolio/Visual Presentation Award), the Society of News Design (Excellence), and the Society of Professional Journalists - Sunshine State Awards.


Before that, I was a local multimedia reporter with Al Día/The Dallas Morning News, covering the Latino community. Our special project on migrant kids from El Salvador won a First Amendment Award from the Society of Professional Journalists (Dallas-Fort Worth).


I began my journalism career in Caracas, Venezuela, as an intern at my hometown newspaper, El Nacional, where I eventually became a staff writer. My coverage included the dismantling of the country's foreign service with the arrival of the Chavista government. Later, I joined the newspaper's investigative unit.


I hold master's degrees in International Affairs and Journalism from Columbia University, where I was a fellow at the Toni Stabile Center for Investigative Journalism. I live in Florida with my husband, 9-year-old twins, and our puppy, Cocosette.
